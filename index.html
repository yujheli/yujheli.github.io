Yu-Jhe (Jack）Li

My name is Yu-Jhe Li, chinese name: 李宇哲, who is currently a PhD candidate in Electrical and Computer Engineering at Carnegie Mellon University (CMU) under the guidance of Prof. Kris Kitani. Prior to my doctoral studies, I worked as a research associate with Kris Kitani at the Robotics Institute for one year.

Additionally, I completed my Master of Science degree at National Taiwan University (國立台灣大學) in 2019, where I was supervised by Prof. Yu-Chiang Frank Wang in the Vision and Learning Lab (VLL). I earned my Bachelor of Science degree from National Tsing Hua University (國立清華大學) in 2017. Furthermore, I have had the opportunity to spend two memorable summers with Meta Research in 2021 and 2022.

    Research

    My research interests span the fields of Machine Learning (ML) and Computer Vision (CV), particularly:

  • Visual representation learning in machine perception (object detection, segmentation, tracking, re-identification).

  • Unsupervised domain adapted learning and data/label efficiency learning in machine perception.

  • Visual representation disentanglement (image/text) for latent-based 2D/3D generative models.

  • Cross-modality 3D human pose reconstruction for XR applications.
